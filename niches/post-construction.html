Sound Familiar?

Post-construction cleaning is profitable — until the chaos eats your margins.

You’re not just cleaning. You’re project managing, risk managing, and putting out fires — all while trying to actually run a business.

📅

Builders Change Timelines Weekly — and Expect You to Just Adapt

The rough clean was supposed to be Monday. Now it’s Thursday. No — wait, next Monday. Your crew is already booked elsewhere, but the builder doesn’t care. You’re constantly reshuffling schedules, burning fuel, and losing jobs because you can’t keep up with someone else’s chaos.

📋

Multi-Phase Cleans with No System to Track Progress

Rough clean, final clean, touch-up — each phase has different requirements, different timelines, and different sign-off criteria. You’re tracking it all in your head, in scattered text messages, or in a spreadsheet that’s already three versions behind. One missed phase and the builder’s on the phone.

⚠️

Safety Compliance Paperwork Eating Into Profitable Cleaning Time

SWMS, site inductions, PPE logs, hazard reports — construction sites demand documentation that residential jobs never will. Every hour your team spends on paperwork is an hour they’re not cleaning. And if an auditor shows up and you can’t produce the records? That’s a problem money can’t fix.

💰

Billing Disputes Because Scope Changes Aren’t Documented

“We never agreed to that.” Four words that cost you thousands. The builder asked for extra window cleaning on-site. Your crew did it. Now nobody remembers the conversation and you’re eating the cost. Without documented scope changes, every invoice becomes a negotiation.

👥

Coordinating Large Crews Across Multiple Active Construction Sites

You’ve got 15 people spread across 4 sites, and each site has different access rules, different contact people, and different completion deadlines. One crew member goes to the wrong address. Another can’t get through the gate. By lunchtime, you’ve lost half a day to coordination failures.
